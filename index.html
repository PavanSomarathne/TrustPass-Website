Proving an individual's identity to a third party via a digital medium has been one of the most discussed topics over the last decade. According to the United States Census Bureau, the world's population was 7.5 billion in June 2019, up from 7.2 billion in 2015. Each of these citizens must establish their identity in order to go about their daily lives. Since the proliferation of internet access, people have tended to use online services rather than sending traditional emails. In Sri Lanka, more than 45% of people use the internet for daily activities, but there is no effective way for public or private sector organizations to utilize the internet for work. Individuals were required to repeatedly enter the same information in order to obtain different services from different institutions or the same institution

This revolutionary technological evolution is causing the entire world to undergo a transformation toward digitalization as a result of its effects. Completing the same information over and over again is tedious because people nowadays lead extremely hectic and stressful lives, despite the fact that they are constantly on the lookout for new and innovative ways to complete tasks that are both quick and dependable in their execution

Many digital identity platforms have emerged in recent decades as a result of the expansion of internet coverage and speed, which have been implemented in a number of countries and smart cities. Taking into consideration the current state of Sri Lanka's digital infrastructure as a country, we have identified key factors that users cared about the most: an integrated platform that can be used to conduct their daily activities without causing them any inconvenience while maintaining their privacy and data security.

In day-to-day activities, people have to verify their identity on certain occasions. Relying Parties use different methods to verify users like physical presence and fill out forms. So, users have to carry at least their national identity and they have to fill forms for their specific requirements. If a National Id or any other valuable item is lost on the go. The process of getting it back costs about a minimum of one week in Sri Lanka because few documents need to be collected in order to get the NIC. RPs will not be able to provide the services if users have no proper documents to validate themselves.

By paving thoughts toward the digital transformation of Sri Lanka, our intention is to create a decentralized digital Identity Platform that can be accessed from anywhere from any citizen that has enhanced security through cyber threat intelligence and threat intrusion analysis system. Our three-factor biometric feature extends the User side authorization to a next level on the other hand time and integrity of the Service providers would be saved with the automated document validator. All of the users will be secured using blockchain technology to minimize the threat from attackers while implementing user privacy.
